There is a growing awareness about the importance of safe sex, and it's important that we all understand how to protect ourselves and our partners. Vaginal ring

This is a small ring made of soft plastic that is placed in the vagina it should be left in the vagina for 21 days then remove and put it in the bin. Seven days after removing the ring. A new one should be inserted for 21 days.

Spermicide

It is a type of contraceptive substance that destroys sperm when inserted vaginally prior to the intercourse to prevent pregnancy from happening.

There are a lot of contraception to use and they were develop to prevent pregnancy and reduce the risk of getting STI. But it is very confusing so it's no surprise that theres people having unprotected intercourse , and it's estimated that 40% of those people get pregnant and an estimate of 1m get STIs being caught worldwide.

The Pill

The widely taken contraceptive pill used by women has a 99% effectiveness when taken properly against unwanted pregnancy. There's 2 types of pills COC pills should be taken every day, or 21 days out of 28 days in a woman's menstrual cycle. She will have 7 day period, before the next set of pills begins.POP pills is another common pill which must be taken with no breaks. It's high effectiveness relies heavily on these stipulations.

IUS

This is a long term method of contraceptive. Lasting around 5-10 years it involves plastic device being inserted into the the uterus to stop ovulation, and has effective of 99% prevention of birthrate.

Diaphragms

This contraceptive works by keeping the egg apart from sperms and should be inserted in the vagina before sex covering the cervix.When used with spermicide and when used correctly the birth rate is around 92-96%. However you must leave it in the vagina for 6 hours after intercourse to be effective.Some women can get cystitis and unlike condoms there a higher chance on getting STIs.

The withdrawal method

Do not use this method because it has the highest chance to getting pregnant or STI 22%.

Contraceptive patch

This contraceptive patch can be placed anywhere on the body as long as it is clean dry and not hairy. A patch should be placed once a week for 3 weeks. During the patch free week you'll get a withdrawal bleed to a period, but it might always not occur.While it is very easy to use it may cause soreness skin irritation and will not protect against STI.

Condoms

This has a 98% protection against unwanted pregnancy and will protect against STI and is the only few who protects from both STI and pregnancy.
